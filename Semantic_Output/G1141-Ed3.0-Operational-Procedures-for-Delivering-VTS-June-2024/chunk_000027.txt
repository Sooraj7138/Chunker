5.1.14. DIGITAL MARITIME SERVICES
Where the VTS provides information in digital format, processes and procedures on digital information exchange should be established.
This information may include, for example, the use of AIS messages to provide information on weather, virtual AtoN or the exchange of route information between vessels and VTS.
5.1.14. DIGITAL MARITIME SERVICES
The procedures should include a description of the digital information delivered and the communication means used for the information exchange.

5.2. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
The activities of the VTS centre should be maintained during any emergency response.
5.2. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
Other authorities (e.g., Maritime Rescue Co-ordination Centre (MRCC)) might be responsible for handling external emergencies within the VTS area.
In that case, procedures for the interaction between the VTS and these authorities need to be established.
5.2. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
Incidental co‐operation with emergency services, such as Search and Rescue and Pollution Control may be conducted in accordance with pre‐established contingency plans in which the procedures for interaction with allied or other services are laid down and responsibilities established.
5.2. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
Despite this, the VTS may be one of the first to respond to a call from a vessel that has an emergency situation.
It is important that VTS requests and collects any further information to help in response activities and to pass on to other authorities.
Some considerations for information that a VTS may try to establish from the vessel under these situations can be found in annex B.

5.2.5. VESSEL NOT UNDER COMMAND (NUC)

5.2.2. POLLUTION
Processes and procedures in the event of a “vessel not under command” should be established.
Actions may include:

5.2.2. POLLUTION
Promulgate information concerning incident to vessels in the VTS area;

5.2.2. POLLUTION

• Obtain detailed information about onboard situation;

5.2.2. POLLUTION

• Maintain communication with vessel;

5.2.2. POLLUTION
Assess vessel’s proximity to danger (danger to vessel itself and other traffic); and

5.2.2. POLLUTION
Alert allied services and other support units, if appropriate.
