4. ESTABLISHING
Competencies within the international framework for establishing a VTS Topic Element 
• Establishing appropriate standards for shore and offshore based equipment.

• Ensuring the VTS Provider is provided with sufficient staff, appropriately qualified, suitably trained and capable of performing the tasks required.

• Establishing appropriate qualifications and training requirements for VTS personnel.

• Instructing the VTS Provider to operate the VTS in accordance with relevant IMO Resolutions.
The principal components of the IALA document structure relating to VTS including: 
• Standards relating to VTS, that is: 
• S1040 Vessel Traffic Services [9] ; 
• S1010 Marine Aids to Navigation Planning and Service Requirements [10] ; 
• S1050 Training and Certification [11] ; and IALA Standards 
• S1070 Information Services [12] .

• Recommendations – In particular, the practices that shall be carried out in order to comply with a recommendation.

• Guidelines – In particular, how to implement practices normally specified in a recommendation.

• Model Courses - training documents which define the level of training and knowledge needed to reach levels of competence defined by IALA.
National law, including the regulatory, compliance and enforcement framework within the context of VTS to: 
• Ensure that a legal basis for the operation of a VTS is provided for and that the VTS is operated in accordance with national and international law.
