1. INTRODUCTION
An efficient and effective vessel traffic service (VTS) relies upon successfully organizing human resources, equipment, systems and facilities as well as the interactions with all parties involved.

1. INTRODUCTION
Recognizing that VTS providers operate under different environments (for example, VTS as a component of a large department/organization, or VTS as a standalone entity) the regulatory and management framework for a particular VTS provider may vary.
Management responsibilities may be delegated to one person or shared between more than one person.
The cooperation of all people involved is required to ensure a smooth and consistent operation.

1. INTRODUCTION
For the purpose of this guidance VTS management is defined as undertaking activities appropriate to achieving the purpose and operational objectives set for the VTS.
These activities may involve, among others, organizing the human, financial, logistic, and technical resources.
