ANNEX A EXAMPLES FOR SETTING OBJECTIVES AND MEASURING THEM
Qualifications and Training VTS personnel should only be considered competent when appropriately trained and qualified for their VTS duties.
This includes: .1 satisfactorily completing generic VTS training approved by a competent authority; .2 satisfactorily completing on‐the‐job training at the VTS where the personnel are employed; .3 undergoing periodic assessments and revalidation training to ensure competence is maintained; and .4 being in possession of appropriate certification.
(Refer A.XXX(XX) Section 8) Quality Management All staff are subject to Quality Management System (QMS) (Refer IALA Recommendation R0132 Quality Management for Aids to Navigation Authority) Establish training policy and procedures for VTS personnel (e.g., number of days, quality of course, etc.) to meet Recommendation R0103. To establish and maintain a formal QMS.

• 100% VTSOs trained to the minimum of V‐103/1 and V‐103/3 
• Recurrent training of VTS personnel completed every 3 years.

• Annual regular assessments of VTS personnel.

• 100% VTS Supervisors trained to the minimum of V‐ 103/1, V‐103/2 and V‐103/3 
• 100% VTS personnel are familiar with the QMS sections relevant to their VTS.
