5. DEFINITIONS
Inland VTS Inland vessel traffic service (inland VTS) – a service in inland waters implemented by an authority with the capability to interact with vessel traffic and respond to developing situations within a vessel traffic service area to improve the safety and efficiency of navigation, contribute to safety of life and support the protection of the environment.
(Adapted from the definition in the new IMO Resolution to apply to inland waters) Inland waters/Inland Inland waters or inland waterways are rivers, lakes or other stretches of water, whether waterways linked to the sea or landlocked, which by natural or man-made features are suitable for navigation.
(UNECE resolution No.
58, Guidelines and Criteria for Vessel Traffic Services on Inland Waterways) Inland waterway Inland waterway vessel is a vessel intended solely or mainly for navigation on inland vessel waterways.
(UNECE resolution No.
61, Recommendations on Harmonized Europe-Wide Technical Requirements for Inland Navigation Vessels, revision 2) Vessel Tracking and Vessel Tracking and Tracing (VTT) combines the functions of tracking and tracing as Tracing (VTT) follows: 
• Vessel Tracking means the function of maintaining status information of the vessel, such as the current position and characteristics and – if needed – combined with information on cargo and consignments.

• Vessel Tracing means the retrieving of information concerning the whereabouts of the vessel and, if needed, information on cargo, consignments and equipment.
(UNECE resolutions Nos.
57, Guidelines and Recommendations for River Information Services, revised, and 63)
