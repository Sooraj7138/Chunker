4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS
At least the following basic information should be included in casualty/incident/near-miss VTS report:

4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS

• Who and what were involved (e.g., vessel data)

4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS

• What happened (e.g., grounding, striking, collision, close quarter situation)

4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS

• Where, when, and in what sequence

4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS

• Weather and hydrographic information

4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS
VTS operator and/or VTS supervisor on duty

4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS
Regardless of the nature of the casualty/incident/near-miss, the additional materials and/or VTS records may be enclosed, such as photographs, traffic image recordings, logs, etc.
4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS
It is advisable to keep a VTS report format as simple as possible.
See example of near-miss/incident/casualty VTS report format in annex B.
4.4. CONTENT OF REPORTS
The procedure of casualty notification is clearly defined in Chapter 5 of Res.MSC.255 (84) known as Casualty Investigation Code, including format and content of such notification.

• Further investigation.

Emergency notification.

Improvement of a VTS decision support tool.

5. BENEFITS OF CASUALTY/INCIDENTS/NEAR-MISS REPORTS
Casualty reports together with relevant recorded VTS information can be used by Marine Safety Investigation Authority for further investigation (according to MSC.255(84) Casualty Investigation Code).

5. BENEFITS OF CASUALTY/INCIDENTS/NEAR-MISS REPORTS
Operational/law enforcement authorities may be interested in receiving information regarding marine incidents/casualties in their area of responsibility as soon as possible.

5. BENEFITS OF CASUALTY/INCIDENTS/NEAR-MISS REPORTS
Incident reports can serve as a source of experience for all stakeholders (VTS personnel, pilots, shipping companies, etc.) and contribute to safety culture.

5. BENEFITS OF CASUALTY/INCIDENTS/NEAR-MISS REPORTS
VTS providers and stakeholders can take into consideration the positive learning from casualty, incident and near- miss reports.
These reports provide opportunities to how the VTS may further improve the safety and efficiency of navigation, safety of life at sea and the protection of the marine environment and/or the adjacent shore area, worksites and offshore installations from possible adverse effects.
