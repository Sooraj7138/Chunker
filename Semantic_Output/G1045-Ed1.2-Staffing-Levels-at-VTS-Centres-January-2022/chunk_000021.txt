5. DEFINITIONS
VTS provider VTS provider means the organization or entity authorized by the Government or competent authority to provide a vessel traffic service.
VTS centre A VTS centre is the centre from which the VTS is operated.
Each sub-area of the VTS may have its own sub-centre.
VTS Manager Some VTS organizations may require the appointment of a manager to administer and interface with regional or port management authorities.
In such circumstances the manager should possess managerial qualifications to the satisfaction of the competent authority.
VTS Operator (VTSO) A VTS Operator is an appropriately qualified person performing one or more tasks contributing to the services of the VTS.
VTS personnel VTS personnel means persons performing tasks associated with vessel traffic services, trained in vessel traffic services operations and appropriately qualified.
VTS Supervisor An appropriately qualified VTSO carrying out supervisory duties in a VTS Centre on behalf of a VTS provider.
VTS traffic image A VTS traffic image is the surface picture of vessels and their movements in a VTS area.
VTS workstation A VTS workstation is the place in a VTS Centre from which VTS personnel carry out their duties.
Watchkeeping Watchkeeping standards, including punctuality, handover, general responsibilities on watch and log-keeping.
Technical watchkeeping requirements at a VTS workstation, include standard target tracking routines, VHF/DF monitoring and checks, navigation and operational checks, and equipment performance checks.
