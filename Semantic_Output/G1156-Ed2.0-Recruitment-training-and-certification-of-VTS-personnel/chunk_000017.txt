8.1. QUALIFICATION
Qualification is the education, knowledge, understanding, proficiency, skill, experience or any other attribute which competent authority has determined as desirable for performing the duties of the position.
VTS qualifications should be based on the principle that satisfactory results are obtained on completion of the IALA VTS model courses.
8.1. QUALIFICATION
VTS personnel should only be considered competent when appropriately trained and qualified for their VTS duties.
8.1. QUALIFICATION
This includes:

8.1. QUALIFICATION
satisfactorily completing generic VTS training approved by the competent authority;

8.1. QUALIFICATION
satisfactorily completing on-the-job training at the VTS where the person is employed;

8.1. QUALIFICATION

• undergoing performance assessment and revalidation training to ensure competence is maintained; and

8.1. QUALIFICATION

• being in possession of appropriate certification.
8.2. CERTIFICATE / CERTIFICATION
A certificate is a document that confirms that a student has met the requirements for the award of a specific qualification.
The certificate is evidence that VTS personnel are considered competent for their VTS duties (certification).
VTS MODEL COURSE CERTIFICATE
A VTS model course certificate is a document awarded by the accredited training organization, to confirm that a student has successfully completed a VTS model course.
The course certificate should include:

VTS MODEL COURSE CERTIFICATE

VTS MODEL COURSE CERTIFICATE
VTS model course certificates should be in the official language or languages of the awarding country.
If the language is not English, then a translation should be available.
VTS MODEL COURSE CERTIFICATE
The use of the IALA logo on a VTS model course certificate recognises that the competent authority is a member of IALA and has approved the delivery of that VTS model course by the accredited training organization in accordance with IALA Guideline G1014.

8.5. VALIDITY OF CERTIFICATES
To continue to be considered competent for their VTS duties, VTS personnel should undergo periodic assessments and revalidation training.
A qualification should remain valid until either:

8.5. VALIDITY OF CERTIFICATES

• an assessment indicates that the holder has fallen below the required competency and proficiency set by the relevant authority;

8.5. VALIDITY OF CERTIFICATES
•

8.5. VALIDITY OF CERTIFICATES
there is a break in service, for any reason, for a period defined by the relevant authority; or

8.5. VALIDITY OF CERTIFICATES
the maximum time period for undertaking revalidation training has been reached.
8.5. VALIDITY OF CERTIFICATES
If a VTS Operator is no longer considered competent for the VTS duties, the Competent Authority should identify a process for managing the situation and revalidating qualifications.
8.5. VALIDITY OF CERTIFICATES
The process for maintaining qualifications is provided in Section 9.
