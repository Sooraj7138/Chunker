6. TRAINING The provision of VTS relies on VTS personnel being appropriately trained and qualified for their VTS duties.
VTS training is supported by five IALA model courses that describe the required training, knowledge, and competence levels.

• C0103-1 - VTS Operator training [6] 
• C0103-2 - VTS Supervisor training [7] 
• C0103-3 - VTS On‐the‐Job training [8] 
• C0103-4 - VTS On‐the‐Job Training Instructor [9] 
• C0103-5 - Revalidation Training for VTS Personnel [10] C0103-1 VTS OPERATOR TRAINING The VTS operator course provides the student with the required knowledge, skills and attitude to undertake the duties associated with the provision of a VTS.
This includes: 
• provide timely and relevant information on factors that may influence the transit of a ship and assist on-board decision making; 
• monitor and manage traffic to ensure the safety and efficiency of ship movements; and respond to developing unsafe situations to assist the decision-making process on board.
C0103-2 VTS SUPERVISOR TRAINING The VTS supervisor course provides a qualified VTS operator with the required knowledge, skills and attitude to undertake the duties of a VTS Supervisor.
This includes: 
• provide leadership and supervision of VTS Operators; 
• carry out administrative duties such as managing the roster, ensuring reporting activities are completed (shift handovers, incident logs/reports).; co-ordinate effective interaction between the VTS, allied and emergency services; support on-the-job training and periodic assessments; and 
• provide guidance during the response to developing unsafe situations and internal / external emergencies.
C0103-3 VTS ON-THE-JOB TRAINING On-the-job (OJT) training is intended to provide the required knowledge, practical competence, skills and attitude to undertake duties at the VTS when they are employed.
IALA Guideline G1156 Recruitment, Training and Certification of VTS Personnel Edition 2.0 urn:mrn:iala:pub:g1156:ed2.0 P 9 The OJT programme covers: Communication Coordination and Interaction 
• Legal Framework 
• Provision of VTS 
• Local Knowledge 
• Equipment 
• Human Factors Emergency Situations VTS providers should determine when OJT training is undertaken to best meet their operational requirements.
For example, OJT training may be completed: 
• prior to the trainee attending formal C0103-1 VTS operator training; 
• after a trainee attends formal C0103-1 VTS operator training; or 
• in multiple stages, with some training prior to, and after completing formal C0103-1 VTS operator training.
C0103-4 VTS ON-THE-JOB TRAINING INSTRUCTOR The VTS on-the-job training instructor course covers the knowledge, skills and attitude required to undertake the duties associated with the role of a VTS OJT Instructor.
On completion of this course the student should have developed their practical skills to provide and coordinate local training (e.g.
OJT, adaption training or updating training) at the VTS centre.
This includes: 
• Designing training programmes including task books and assessment materials.
Learning teaching and instructional techniques to deliver training programmes.
Providing feedback to learners.
Assessing the required competence of a student against the training objectives.
Evaluating the effectiveness and continually improving training programmes.
C0103-5 REVALIDATION TRAINING FOR VTS PERSONNEL This course provides a structured means for VTS personnel to demonstrate the ongoing knowledge, practical competence, skills, and attitude to continue to undertake the duties associated with the provision of VTS.
Upon the successful completion of this course, VTS personnel should have demonstrated the skill, knowledge and experience to revalidate their VTS qualifications in order to perform their roles effectively.
ACCREDITATION AND APPROVAL OF VTS MODEL COURSES The competent authorities need to ensure that training organisations are accredited and approved for each IALA VTS model course that they deliver.
This process involves two steps: IALA Guideline G1156 Recruitment, Training and Certification of VTS Personnel Edition 2.0 urn:mrn:iala:pub:g1156:ed2.0 P 10 
• Accreditation which is the formal endorsement that a training organization operates under a suitable quality management system to deliver effective training.

• Approval which is the formal endorsement that a training organization meets the standards specified in an IALA model course for its implementation, delivery and assessment.
IALA Guideline G1014 Accreditation of VTS Training Organizations and Approval to Deliver IALA VTS Model Courses [11] sets out the process by which a training organization can be accredited to deliver approved VTS training courses.
A list of training organizations accredited to deliver VTS model courses in accordance with IALA Guideline G1014 are available on the IALA website.
