2.8.2. TELEPHONE AND OTHER COMMUNICATIONS
The VTS Centre is the heart of a complex web of information, which can result in the reception of a large number of enquiries by telephone not directly connected with the VTS function.
Each telephone call made or received by VTS personnel generates workload.
2.8.2. TELEPHONE AND OTHER COMMUNICATIONS
Where VTS Centres have responsibility for tasks additional to vessel traffic services, staffing levels may need to be enhanced accordingly.
VTS providers should develop a clear policy on the nature of telephone communication to and from the VTS Centre.
2.8.2. TELEPHONE AND OTHER COMMUNICATIONS
One way of reducing telephone communication is to ensure that appropriate means are available to facilitate the access of information to relevant parties concerned (i.e., through a computer network or the Internet).
