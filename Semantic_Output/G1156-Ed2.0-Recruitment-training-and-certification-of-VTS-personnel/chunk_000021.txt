9.4. UNSTRUCTURED LEARNING
Unstructured (sometimes called informal) learning occurs through everyday on-the-job experiences, interactions, and activities, without the need for formal training programs or instruction.
It is typically unplanned and spontaneous, where individuals gain knowledge, skills, or insights through:

9.4. UNSTRUCTURED LEARNING

• On-the-job learning where new skills or knowledge are gained by observing, trying tasks themselves, or receiving guidance from others.
9.4. UNSTRUCTURED LEARNING

• Peer discussions where insights are informally exchanged such as in the review and analysis of lessons learned from local VTS operations or incidents.
9.4. UNSTRUCTURED LEARNING

• Self-directed learning where an individual explores new topics, skills, or tools on their own, such as reading articles, watching videos/tutorials, or experimenting with software functionality.
9.4. UNSTRUCTURED LEARNING

• Trial and error where skills are gained by experimenting with different approaches, learning from mistakes, and improving through repeated practice.
